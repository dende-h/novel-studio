執筆中

第四章：記憶の建築

The city did not grow organically. It was constructed, layer by deliberate layer, upon the bedrock of forgotten iterations. Architect Elias Thorne sat at his drafting table, the harsh light of the solitary lamp casting long, distorted shadows across the vellum. He traced the lines of the old district, noting where the streets veered inexplicably, bending around structures that no longer existed.

He had spent the better part of the decade attempting to map these negative spaces. They held a resonance, a quiet vibration that one could feel only in the dead of night when the modern machinery ceased its rhythmic hum. The locals whispered about the 'echo-forms,' spectral buildings that appeared in the periphery of vision when the mist rolled in off the estuary.

Tonight, however, his focus was drawn to a specific anomaly near the central plaza. According to the archives, the square had always been open, designed for public gatherings and the solstice festivals. Yet, his latest cross-referencing with ground-penetrating radar data suggested a massive, submerged foundation right in the center. A foundation that matched no known historical record of the city.
